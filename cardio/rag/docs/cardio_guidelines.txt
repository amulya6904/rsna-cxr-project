Cardiology Knowledge Base

High blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, smoking, obesity, family history, and sedentary lifestyle are major cardiovascular risk factors.

Patients with high cardiovascular risk should be advised to follow up with a cardiologist, monitor blood pressure regularly, improve diet, reduce saturated fats, exercise regularly, stop smoking, and follow prescribed medication.

ECG abnormalities, chest pain, shortness of breath, fatigue, palpitations, and high cholesterol may indicate increased risk of cardiovascular disease.

Risk prediction should not be used as a final diagnosis. It should support clinical decision-making and must be reviewed by a qualified healthcare professional.
